### Stable Audio Open — the $1M ARR cap

The Stability AI Community License permits commercial use as long as your organization earns **less than $1M USD in total annual revenue** (across all products, not just ones built on Stable Audio). Above that threshold you must:
1. Stop commercial use, OR
2. Negotiate a commercial license directly with Stability AI.

Revenue is calculated on a trailing 12-month basis. The license includes a "responsible AI use" appendix.

Source: https://stability.ai/license

For users above the cap or who need a cleaner boundary, use **Riffusion** (MIT) or **YuE** (Apache 2.0) instead — both are unconditionally commercial-safe.

## Output copyright

Music generated by these models is under the same permissive license as the model — you own usage rights. However:

1. **Melody-copyright risk:** AI music generators can inadvertently reproduce copyrighted melodies. If your prompt explicitly references an artist or song ("make it sound like \<artist\>"), the risk goes up. Prefer genre / mood / instrumentation descriptors.
2. **Vocal / lyric copyright:** YuE generates vocals with lyrics. If you supply lyrics from a copyrighted source, the output inherits that copyright issue.
3. **Training-data disclosure:** None of these models publish full training-set lists. Commercial deployment in regulated industries (games with union voice actors, films with PRO-registered music) may require additional clearance.
4. **Platform ToS:** Spotify / YouTube / SoundCloud each have their own AI-generated-music disclosure policies — check per-platform.

## Safe-default recommendations

- **Need it to work commercially with zero license surface?** Use **Riffusion** (MIT) for instrumental, **YuE** (Apache 2.0) for full songs with vocals.
- **Need best audio quality and revenue < $1M?** Stable Audio Open is fine.
- **Need best quality at $1M+ ARR?** Contact Stability for a commercial license, OR stay with Riffusion / YuE.
